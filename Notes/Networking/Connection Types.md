**Ethernet**
A wired connection commonly used in LANs providing high-speed, stable and secure data transfer.
Data tends to diminish after 100 meters.

**Wi-Fi**
A wireless connection that uses radio waves to transmit data to devices. Providing flexibility and mobility  making it good for devices that can move around, phones, laptops, etc.

**Bluetooth**
Short ranges wireless technology mostly used for connecting peripherals such as headphones or speakers.
Operates over short distances usually up to 10 meters.

**Fiber-optic**
Uses light signals through glass or plastic fibers to transmit data at very high speeds over long distances, mostly used for internet backbones or connecting data centers.

**Cellular connections**
Wireless cellular networks that allow mobile devices to connect to the internet.

**NFC**
A short-ranged wireless technology that allows devices to communicate and exchange data over very short distances, (up to 4 inches). Commonly used for contactless payments, electronic ticketing, etc.
Operates at a frequency of 13.56MHz

**Infrared (IR)**
A type of wireless communication technology that utilizes light waves in electromagnetic spectrum to transmit data between devices.
