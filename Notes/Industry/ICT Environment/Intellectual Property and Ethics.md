# Key Concepts
**Organisational IP**
Protects the creations and inventions of the organisation

**Ethics**
Guidelines that defines acceptable behaviours within the organisation

**Privacy Policy Procedures**
Measures to protect personal data and ensure compliance with privacy laws

# Principles
Foundational values and standards that guide how an organisation protects IP, support ethical conduct and maintains privacy.
- Promotes respect for ownership of ideas and creations
- Encourages ethical decision making and integrity
- Enforces compliance of laws and protection of personal data
- Promotes accountability and fairness
- Aligns with legal and regulatory frameworks
# Intellectual Property (IP) Legislation
IP legislation protects creations of the mind and ensures ownership rights are respected.

**In Australia this includes:**
- Copyright Act 1968
- Patents Act 1990
- Trade Marks Act 1995
- Designs Act 2003
- International agreements

**IP legislation governs:**
- Who owns digital creations
- How others may use or access them
- Penalties for infringement
- Enforcement of licensing conditions
# What is Intellectual Property (IP)
IP refers to creations of the mind such as designs, software, written content, brand names and inventions.

IP give the creator or owner the exclusive right to:
- Use, share, sell
- License, control how work is used by others

Intellectual Property:
- Protects unique creations
- Creates a competitive advantage
- Prevents unauthorised use of code, images or digital assets
- Ensures compliance with copyright and licensing rules
- Helps organisations manage purchased software rights
- Supports ethical and legal behavior in technology environments
# IP Rights
IP laws protect the rights of creators and provide them with control over their work.
# Types of IP
**Copyright**
- Protects original creative works
- Gives creators exclusive right to copy, share or modify their work
**Patents**
- Protects new inventions, designed and technical processes
- Give inventors exclusive rights to make or sell the invention
**Trademarks**
- Protects brand identifiers like names, logos, symbols and slogans
- Helps customers recognise a company or products
# Organisational Policy and Procedures
Organisations establish policies to ensure consistent and compliant behavior.

These may include:
- Acceptable use of ICT systems
- Software licensing and procurement rules
- Copyright and IP guidelines
- Data privacy and security procedures
# Adherence to Policies and Procedures
**ICT staff must:**
- Follow all documented procedures
- Apply legislation correctly in daily tasks
- Protect organisations IP and digital assets
- Report breaches or risks immediately
# Legal and Ethical Considerations
**ICT professionals must follow:**
- Privacy laws
- Workplace policies
- Copyright legislation
- IP ownership rules
- Cybersecurity requirements
- Duty of care obligations
