# Key Concepts
- **Organisational IP Requirements:** Policies that guide how staff protect and use the organisations IP
- **Industry-Standard Legislation:** Laws that govern how IP must be used and protected
- **Work Practice Compliance:** Ensuring daily tasks follow organisational policies and legal requirements
# Organisational IP Requirements
IP requirements must align with both internal policies and legislation.
- Protect digital assets
- Define correct usage of information and systems
- Ensure all staff comply with legal obligations
- Reduce risks around copyright, licensing and data misuse
# Organisational Policies
Defines how staff must behave when using digital tools and handling IP
**Common organisational policies include:**
- Acceptable User Policy (AUP)
- IP and copyright policy
- Software licensing policy
- Data protection and privacy policy
- Code of conduct and ethics
- Security and access control procedures
# Legislation
These laws influence workplace practices and dictate what is legally permissible.
**Industry-standard legislation may include:**
- Copyright Act 1968
- Patents Act 1990
- Trade Marks Act 1995
- Privacy Act 1988 (including Australian Privacy Principles)
- Designs Act 2003
- Software licensing terms (open-source and commercial)
# Comparing Policy to work practices
This ensures the organisation is protected and compliant
**When identifying IP requirements you must:**
- Review current organisational practices
- Compare them with internal policies
- Check whether work tasks align with legal obligations
- Identify any risks or gaps
- Determine where changes or reminders are needed
# Examples of misalignment
These issues expose the organisation to legal and ethical risks.
**Misalignment occurs when:**
- Staff use unlicensed or pirated software
- External images or code are used without permission
- Sensitive documents are shared outside approved channels
- Branding is applied incorrectly
- Staff store files in personal cloud accounts
# Document Findings
Documentation should be clear, accurate and easy for stakeholders to understand
**When documenting organisations IP requirements include:**
- Relevant policy names
- Applicable laws or standards
- Which tasks or roles each requirement affects
- Risks identified
- Required updates or corrective actions
# Why documenting matters
**Identifying and documenting IP requirements:**
- Ensures operational tasks comply with laws
- Reduce organisational risk
- Supports ethical and professional ICT practice
- Helps teams understand their responsibilities
- Creates a foundation for training and policy updates
